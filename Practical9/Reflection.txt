This practical was quite interesting. I found the basic SIR model easy to implement, 
but the spatial version was more challenging due to handling neighbor indices and 
simultaneous updates. The most enjoyable part was seeing the disease spread visually 
in the 2D model. I also learned that even small changes in beta or gamma can 
dramatically affect the outbreak. The vaccination extension helped me understand 
herd immunity intuitively. Overall, it was a valuable hands-on experience with 
stochastic modeling.
